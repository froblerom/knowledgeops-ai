# Customer Support Refund & Escalation Policy

## Standard Refund Window

Customers may request a refund within **14 days of confirmed delivery**. To qualify, the item must be unused, in its original packaging, and accompanied by proof of purchase. Refund requests submitted within the standard window are processed within 5 business days of item receipt.

Agents should confirm the delivery date from the order management system before accepting a refund request. If the delivery date cannot be confirmed, the request must be escalated to a supervisor before any commitment is made to the customer.

## Damaged Item Claims

Customers reporting damage to a received item must submit a damage claim within **7 days of delivery**. The following documentation is required before the claim can be processed:

- A clear photograph of the damaged item and its packaging.
- A written description of the damage.
- The original order number and delivery confirmation.

Agents must not approve a refund or replacement on the first contact for damaged item claims. The claim must be reviewed and approved by a supervisor or the claims team before any resolution is offered to the customer.

## Out-of-Window Cases (15–30 Days)

Refund requests submitted between **15 and 30 days after delivery** are considered out-of-window cases and require supervisor authorization before any refund is issued or committed to the customer.

For damaged item claims submitted between 15 and 30 days after delivery, the agent must:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the customer's report.
2. Collect all required documentation (photograph, description, order number).
3. Escalate the case to a supervisor for review before offering any resolution.
4. Never promise a refund, replacement, or credit without supervisor approval.

Cases submitted beyond 30 days after delivery are generally ineligible for a standard refund and must be referred to the Senior Support team for case-by-case review.

## Before Offering a Refund

Before offering a refund or any compensation to a customer, the agent must confirm all of the following:

- The purchase date and delivery confirmation have been verified.
- The case falls within the eligible refund window or has received explicit supervisor authorization.
- For damage claims, the required photo and written evidence have been collected.
- The customer's account and order status have been reviewed and are in good standing.
- If the case is out-of-window, supervisor approval has been obtained in writing before communicating any resolution.

Agents are not authorized to approve refunds on their own authority for out-of-window cases, high-value orders above the agent approval threshold, or any case that involves contested delivery.

## Escalation Protocol

Escalate to a supervisor immediately when:

- The customer reports a damaged item and the claim is more than 7 days after delivery.
- The refund request is outside the standard 14-day window.
- The customer is disputing the delivery date or the condition of the item.
- The order value exceeds the agent authorization threshold.
- The customer requests to speak to a supervisor directly.

Document the escalation in the case management system, including the time of escalation, the supervisor assigned, and a brief summary of the customer's claim.

## Required Documentation

All refund and damage claims must be recorded in the case management system with:

- Customer name and account ID.
- Order number and item description.
- Reported issue and customer-provided evidence (photos, written description).
- Delivery date and days since delivery.
- Agent notes and escalation history if applicable.
- Final resolution decision and approving supervisor (if applicable).

Incomplete records may delay resolution and must be completed before the case is closed.
